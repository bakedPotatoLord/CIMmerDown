## Instructions

There are multiple manufacturing methods used to create this board. I will discuss each and provide the files here. feel free to skip around and make the parts in any order you prefer


### 3D printing

The following parts are 3D printed:  BRD-PRN-02,BRD-PRN-01,BRD-PRN-03,BRD-PRN-04,BRD-MCN-05,BRD-WHL-01

These parts are not part of the final product, but are molds and are requred for urethane molding: BRD-WHL-05,BRD-WHL-06

Finally, this part is used as a modifier mesh when 3D printing: BRD-WHL-03

All of these files can be downloaded from as a zip archive in [STL](/assets/STL.zip) or [STEP](/assets/STEP.zip) format, as well as individualy from this github repo.

All of the parts for the board are recommended to be printed in PETG with at least 4 walls. chances are these parts will take a beating, so the many walls are recommended to protect the things on the inside.

The gasket for the electronics box is recommended to be printed in TPU if available, but the box works fine without it. It will just be slightly less water resistant.

The molds for the wheels can be printed in low-infill PLA, or whatever is cheapest for you. 

The modifier mesh can be used on the exterior of both the powered and Unpowered hub to enhance surface area for the urethane to grip. you can learn more about modifier meshes in this [article](https://blog.prusa3d.com/model-with-prusaslicer-modifier-meshes-tutorial_30438/). It will be slightly different based on your slicer.

### Metal Manufacturing

The DXFs for the sheet-cut parts can be found here \[add link\]

My general order of operations was to waterjet out the profiles of the sheet parts, and then drill them out on the manual mill.
the triangle holes need to be cut on a rotary table, or with a CNC mill. These parts are likely small enough for a Desktop Mill if that's all you have access to. 

The spacers could probably be 3D printed if you don't have a lathe. Just make sure they're printed in PETG with max wall loops.
